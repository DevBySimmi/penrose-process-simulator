Penrose Process Simulator

Overview

This project provides a scientific visualization of the Penrose Process occurring inside the ergosphere of a Kerr Black Hole.

The Penrose Process is a theoretical mechanism proposed by physicist Roger Penrose that allows energy to be extracted from a rotating black hole. This simulation demonstrates particle trajectories and the geometry of the Kerr ergosphere using Python.

- Kerr Black Hole Visualization

---

Physics Background

A rotating black hole is described by the Kerr metric.

Inside the ergosphere, spacetime is dragged by the black hole's rotation. Under special conditions, a particle can split into two fragments:

1. One fragment falls into the black hole.
2. The second fragment escapes with more energy than the original particle.

This process effectively extracts rotational energy from the black hole.
